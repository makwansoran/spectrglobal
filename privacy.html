Privacy policy

Last updated: 13 May 2026 · Controller: SPECTR AS (Norway), Org. nr. 926 574 892

1. Who we are

This website is operated by SPECTR AS, a limited company registered in Norway (organisation number 926 574 892), with registered office at Eksempelgata 1, NO-0162 Oslo, Norway (“Spectr”, “we”, “us”). For privacy enquiries, contact makwan@spectr.no.

2. Scope

This policy describes how we process personal data when you use our public website (including forms), communicate with us, or otherwise interact with Spectr in ways covered here. It is intended to meet our obligations under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) as implemented in Norway through the Personal Data Act (personopplysningsloven) and related regulations.

3. What data we collect

Depending on how you use our services, we may process:

  • Website and technical data: IP address, approximate location (derived from IP), browser type, device type, referring URL, pages viewed, and timestamps. This is collected through normal server logs and similar technical mechanisms needed to operate and secure the site.
  • Contact and inquiry data: If you submit our contact or quote form, we process the fields you provide (for example name, work email, company, phone if supplied, service selection, and your free-text message about lanes, volumes, or requirements).
  • Correspondence: Content of emails or other messages you send to addresses we publish (such as makwan@spectr.no).

4. Purposes and legal bases

We use personal data for the following purposes:

  • Responding to inquiries and operating our business (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b) steps prior to contract, and (f) legitimate interests in communicating with prospective customers): processing form submissions and email.
  • Security, fraud prevention, and troubleshooting (legitimate interests, Art. 6(1)(f)): reviewing logs and technical data to protect systems and users.
  • Compliance with law (Art. 6(1)(c)): retaining records where required for tax, accounting, or regulatory obligations.

5. Form delivery and processors

Contact form submissions may be transmitted using a third-party form delivery provider (for example FormSubmit or a successor configured in the site’s form action). That provider processes submissions on our instructions to deliver email to our inbox. Their processing is governed by their terms and privacy policy; you should review those documents on the provider’s site. We choose providers that support appropriate technical measures; you may also contact us directly by email if you prefer not to use the web form.

We may also use an optional webhook or inbox automation (for example Zapier, Make, or Relay) so that each inquiry is copied into our CRM (for example Attio). Those services process data on our instructions under their own terms and privacy policies. We do not embed CRM API credentials in this public website.

6. Cookies and similar technologies

This marketing site is designed to work without non-essential tracking cookies. Essential cookies or local storage may still be used where strictly necessary for security or form functionality. If we introduce analytics or marketing tags that require consent under ePrivacy rules, we will update this policy and, where required, present a consent mechanism before those technologies load.

7. Retention

We retain inquiry and correspondence data for as long as needed to handle your request, manage the commercial relationship that may follow, and meet legal retention requirements (for example bookkeeping). Server logs are retained on a rolling basis typical for security and operations, then deleted or anonymised. Specific retention periods may vary by category; you may ask us for more detail in a particular case.

8. Sharing and transfers

We do not sell your personal data. We may share data with service providers (such as hosting, email, or form delivery) under written agreements where required. If a provider is located outside the European Economic Area, we ensure appropriate safeguards (for example Standard Contractual Clauses) are in place where required by law.

9. Your rights

Subject to Norwegian law and conditions in the GDPR, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you;
  • Rectify inaccurate data;
  • Request erasure, restriction, or object to certain processing;
  • Data portability, where applicable;
  • Withdraw consent where processing was based on consent;
  • Lodge a complaint with the Norwegian Data Protection Authority (Datatilsynet).

To exercise your rights, email makwan@spectr.no. We may need to verify your identity before responding.

10. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against accidental loss, unauthorised access, or disclosure. No method of transmission over the Internet is completely secure; please use discretion when sharing sensitive information.

11. Children

Our services are directed at businesses and adults. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe we have processed a child’s data in error, contact us and we will take appropriate steps.

12. Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top will change when we do. Material changes may be highlighted on the website or communicated where appropriate.
